Estimating London's EU referendum result with Multilevel Regression and Poststratification¶

In the previous notebook we encountered a predominance of missing data for Ward Level EU Referendum Electoral Results. Given the study's initial notebook exploration of a correlation between the 2011 Census Data and EU Refendum Response, we extend this process with Multilevel Regression and Poststratification and PYMC3, as detailed by Austin Roachford's Python exploration of Jonathan Kastellac MRP R Primer - to estimate values for the Missing Ward Data. We then compare our results with Constituency level EU Ref Result data obtained from a subsequent study by Chris Hannretty. We carry out a Disaggregation of the EU Ref Result data to highlight the missing data.

Links to both the Notebooks and Research can be found below.

We will refer to each unique combination of state and demographic characteristics as a cell. Let $n_i$ denote the number of respondents in cell $i$, $y_i$ the number of those respondents that supported the Remaining in the EU and $p_i$ the probability that a member of the general population of cell $i$ supports the Remain Camp. We build a Bayesian multilevel logistic regression model of opinion as follows.

$$\begin{align*} \eta_i & = \beta_0 + \alpha^{\textrm{London Mayoral Pref : Borough Council Hue}}_{j(i)} + \alpha^{\textrm{age}}_{k(i)} + \alpha^{\textrm{edu}}_{l(i)} + \alpha^{\textrm{age : edu}}_{k(i),\ l(i)} + \alpha^{\textrm{state}}_{s(i)} + \alpha^{\textrm{poll}}_{m(i)} \\ \log \left(\frac{p_i}{1 - p_i}\right) & = \eta_i \\ y_i & \sim \textrm{Bernoulli}(n_i, p_i) \end{align*}$$

Here each subscript indexed by $i$ is the categorical level of that characteristic for respondents in cell $i$. The prior for the intercept is $\beta_0 \sim N(0, 5^2)$. The prior for the effects of the interaction of London Mayoral Pref and age is $\alpha^{\textrm{London Mayoral Pref : MP Constituency Party}}_j \sim N\left(0, \sigma_{\textrm{London Mayoral Pref : Borough Council Hue}}^2\right),$ with $\sigma_{\textrm{London Mayoral Pref : MP Constituency Party}} \sim \textrm{HalfCauchy}(5)$. The priors on $\alpha^{\textrm{age}}_k,$ $\alpha^{\textrm{edu}}_l,$ $\alpha^{\textrm{age : edu}}_{k,\ l},$ and $\alpha^{\textrm{Borough Council Hue}}_m$ are defined similarly. The prior on the state term, $\alpha^{\textrm{state}}_s$, includes state-level predictors for Constituency, Percentage Turnout for 2015 General Election, and Percentage Majority for 2015 General Election.

The idea behind this being to introduce Voter Engagement/Enfranchisment into the Model.

$$\begin{align*} \alpha^{\textrm{state}}_s & \sim N\left(\alpha^{\textrm{Constituency}}_s + \beta^{\textrm{Perc Turnout}} x^{\textrm{Perc Turnout}}_s + \beta^{\textrm{Perc Majority}} x^{\textrm{Perc Majority}}_s, \sigma^2_{\textrm{Ward}}\right) \end{align*}$$

Here $x^{\textrm{Perc Turnout}}_s$ is the log odds of the proportion of the ward's residents that turned out to vote at the 2015 General Election, and $x^{\textrm{Perc Majority}}_s$ is the log odds of the Percentage of the Majority secured by the Successful MP. The priors on $\alpha^{\textrm{ward}}_s$, $\beta^{\textrm{Perc Turnout}}$, $\beta^{\textrm{Perc Majority}}$ are the same as those on the analagous terms in the definition of $\eta$.

def highlight_greater(x):
    r = 'red'
    g = 'green'

    m1 = (x['Remain_Vote_Estimation'] > .5) & (x['Actual_Remain_Result'] > .5)
    m2 = (x['Remain_Vote_Estimation'] < .5) & (x['Actual_Remain_Result'] < .5)
    
    #print(m1)

    df1 = pd.DataFrame('background-color: ', index=x.index, columns=x.columns)
    #rewrite values by boolean masks
    df1['Remain_Vote_Estimation'] = np.where(m1, 'background-color: {}'.format(g), df1['Remain_Vote_Estimation'])
    df1['Actual_Remain_Result'] = np.where(m2, 'background-color: {}'.format(r), df1['Actual_Remain_Result'])

    results_join_df['Remain_Vote_Estimation'] = results_join_df['Remain_Vote_Estimation'].astype(float).map("{:.2%}".format)
    results_join_df['Actual_Leave_Result'] = results_join_df['Actual_Leave_Result'].astype(float).map("{:.2%}".format)
    results_join_df['Actual_Remain_Result'] = results_join_df['Actual_Remain_Result'].astype(float).map("{:.2%}".format)
    
    return df1
